# Psycho

!!! danger "Content warnings"
    Violent sequences, particularly the iconic shower scene. Older
    audiences may have been desensitized to it through cultural
    repetition; first-time viewers often aren't.

## Production background

Alfred Hitchcock financed and produced *Psycho* independently after
Paramount Pictures rejected the project, deeming Robert Bloch's
novel "too repulsive" for film. Rather than accept Paramount's
refusal to fund it, Hitchcock offered to shoot the film
economically in black-and-white using his television crew,
proposing to take a percentage of the film's profits instead of his
standard director's fee. This cost-conscious approach proved
remarkably successful — the old pro really poured it on in this
production, resulting in a film made for approximately $807,000
that would generate over $15 million in personal earnings for
Hitchcock.

## Impact on horror cinema

*Psycho* fundamentally transformed the horror genre by establishing
new standards for depicting violence and deviant behavior in
American cinema. The film's critical re-evaluation after its
initial polarized reception helped legitimize psychological horror
as a serious artistic endeavor, inspiring subsequent filmmakers to
explore darker material. Within three years, films like *Blood
Feast* emerged as direct responses to Psycho's influence, launching
what became known as the splatter film movement and demonstrating
the profound and lasting impact Hitchcock's masterwork had on
horror filmmaking.

???+ note "Hitchcock's promotional gimmick"
    Hitchcock made theaters refuse late entry to *Psycho* — once
    the film began, you couldn't come in. He positioned this as
    being for "the audience's protection," but it doubled as a
    demand for attention and a guard against people leaking the
    plot to friends in the lobby.

!!! warning
    *Psycho*'s plot turns are now so culturally embedded that
    "spoiler-free" doesn't really apply. If you've never seen it
    and somehow don't know what happens — go in immediately, before
    that changes.
