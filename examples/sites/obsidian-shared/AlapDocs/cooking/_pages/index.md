## On simplicity

Italian cuisine has long been celebrated for its elegant simplicity.
A key characteristic is that many dishes are made up of few
ingredients, which means cooks rely heavily on the quality of what
they use rather than complicated techniques. This philosophy emerged
organically from everyday people creating meals for their families,
not from chefs working in fancy kitchens.

The regional diversity of Italian cooking reflects the country's
varied landscapes and histories. Each area, from the Alpine valleys
to Mediterranean coasts, developed distinct culinary traditions
based on what grows locally and what trades brought in from
neighboring regions. A dish created in one region may have completely
different variations elsewhere in Italy, yet all versions honor the
principle of letting quality ingredients shine.
