# Pride and Prejudice

> [!note] Reading status
> Read June 2025. Reread early 2026 for the dialogue.

## Setting and stakes

Pride and Prejudice depicts early nineteenth-century English
society during the Napoleonic Wars, focusing on the landed gentry
and their complex economic circumstances. The novel centers on the
Bennet family of Hertfordshire, where five daughters face an
uncertain future because their estate is entailed to the male line.
As the novel opens, "It is a truth universally acknowledged, that a
single man in possession of a good fortune, must be in want of a
wife," establishing marriage as the primary means by which young
women of modest means could secure their financial stability and
social standing.

## Elizabeth and Darcy

The novel's protagonist, Elizabeth Bennet, embodies an
unconventional independence of thought that was considered daring
for her time. She initially despises the wealthy Mr. Darcy for his
apparent arrogance and pride, while he struggles to overcome his
own prejudices against her family's lower social connections and
her mother's embarrassing behavior. Through their interactions and
mutual critiques, both characters gradually recognize their faults
and develop genuine understanding, ultimately discovering that
genuine love requires overcoming both social barriers and personal
misconceptions about each other's true character.

## Editions


  

  Penguin Classics, 2003. Standard scholarly edition with
  introduction by Vivien Jones. The cheapest reliable copy.

  
  

  Oxford World's Classics keeps a hardcover with the same kind of
  apparatus. Better paper; harder to one-hand on the bus.

  
  

  The 2013 Rosamund Pike narration is the consensus pick. Pike
  pitches Mr. Collins's pomposity perfectly without overdoing it.
