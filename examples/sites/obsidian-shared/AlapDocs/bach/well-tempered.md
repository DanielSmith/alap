Each prelude-and-fugue pair is small enough to sit with in one
sitting, but the cumulative effect of the cycle is something else.

## What it is

Bach's monumental collection comprises two sets of twenty-four
preludes and fugues, one for each major and minor key. Each book
presents a systematic journey through all keys in chromatic order,
beginning with C major and its parallel minor before progressing
through each successive half-tone until reaching B minor. This
comprehensive approach was revolutionary for its time, as it
demonstrated that keyboard instruments could be played in all keys
using a well-tempered tuning system rather than the limited
meantone temperament previously in common use.

## Bach's stated purpose

Bach explicitly designed the work as an educational resource, as
evidenced by the title page of the first book, which states the
collection was created "for the profit and use of the studious
musical young, and also for the special diversion of those who are
already skilful in this study." The pedagogical intent did not
diminish the artistic sophistication of the pieces themselves;
rather, Bach created a monument that served both teaching and
artistic purposes. The work's influence on subsequent composers
proved immense, inspiring Chopin to compose his own set of
twenty-four preludes and later prompting Shostakovich to create a
parallel cycle in the twentieth century.
